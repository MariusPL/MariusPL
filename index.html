About Me

I'm an international undergraduate student at UCLA, double majoring in Computational Linguistics and Psychology. I was born in Chile, grew up in China and Taiwan, formed my identity in Argentina, and found myself in Hong Kong. My interests include -- but are not limited to: Reinforcement Learning, NLP (transformers), language acquisition, code optimization. Even though I am an atheist and advocate of science, I enjoy spirituality and art, often engaging in philosophy, music, photography, and architecture.

AI Related Projects

Transformer Black Box Investigation (ongoing)

Collaborating with Buck Shlegeris from Redwood Research, we are trying to find explanations and quantitative differences between the sizes of language models and their performance at next token prediction.

Independent Research on MARL (ongoing, 30%)

Conducting independent research mentored by professor AAA, it is an investigation on social emergence in multi agent cooperative environments. With this project I want to contribute to the ongoing CAI conversation and also explore the idea of natural language emergence.

Distillation Circles (ongoing)

Creating biweekly distillations of state of the art technical AI advancement papers. Drawing connection with past publications and pondering implications for the future.

AIS Knowledge Bank (ongoing, 50%)

I am currently compiling an AI Safety knowledge bank for myself and people I want to introduce to the topic. Including summaries of relevant papers to understand the current discussion landscape
